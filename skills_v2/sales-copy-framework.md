## Framework Selection

Choose framework based on context:
- **PAS** (Problem-Agitate-Solution): cold audience, awareness stage, Facebook ads, email subject lines
- **AIDA** (Attention-Interest-Desire-Action): landing pages, email sequences, product pages
- **BAB** (Before-After-Bridge): testimonial-style sections, case study intros, transformation copy
- **4Ps** (Promise-Picture-Proof-Push): long-form sales pages, webinar registration pages

## PAS Template
```
[PROBLEM] — State the exact pain with specificity
[AGITATE] — Deepen the pain: consequences, missed opportunities, emotional cost
[SOLUTION] — Introduce product/service as the resolution, benefit-first
[CTA] — Single, clear action with urgency or value anchor
```

## CTA Construction Rules
- Format: [Action verb] + [Immediate benefit] + [Optional urgency]
- Examples: "Szerezd meg a sablont", "Kérd az ingyenes konzultációt ma", "Indítsd el a 14 napos próbát"
- Button labels: max 5 words
- Never: "Kattints ide", "Submit", "Küldés" — these are non-descriptive
- Urgency only if genuine: deadline, limited seats, price increase

## Benefit Stack Rules
- Lead with outcome, not feature: "Napi 2 óra munkával" not "automata rendszer"
- Stack 3-5 benefits in bullet form
- Each bullet: one idea, one sentence, starts with verb or number
- Final bullet: the biggest transformation or risk reversal

## Objection Pre-emption
Identify top 3 objections for target audience, address each with:
- Acknowledge: "Tudom, most gondolod..."
- Reframe: evidence, data, analogy
- Redirect: return to benefit

## Output Expectations
- Full copy in chosen framework
- 2 headline variations
- Benefit stack (5 bullets)
- 3 CTA variations (button label + surrounding context)
- Objection-handling block for top 3 objections

## Quality Criteria
- Copy readable at 8th-grade level (short sentences, active voice)
- Every claim specific and substantiatable
- CTA appears at minimum 2× on the page (above fold + end)
