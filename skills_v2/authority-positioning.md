## Authority Building Framework

**Target context:** consulting proposals, about pages, executive bios, thought leadership articles, B2B landing pages

## Credibility Signal Hierarchy (use in order of strength)
1. **Measurable results:** "37 vállalkozás, összesen 2,4 milliárd Ft árbevétel-növekedés"
2. **Named clients or sectors:** "Partnereink között: [iparág] vállalatok"
3. **Methodology name:** proprietary framework signals depth and systematization
4. **Media / publication mentions:** press, podcast, speaking
5. **Credentials and tenure:** years of experience with specific context (not just "10 éve")
6. **Social proof count:** "450+ vállalkozás" — specificity builds trust

## Executive Bio Structure
1. What they achieve for clients (outcome first, not background)
2. Named methodology or signature approach (1 sentence)
3. Quantified track record (1-2 specific metrics)
4. Professional background compressed (2 sentences max)
5. Human dimension (1 sentence — optional, increases relatability)

## Trust Signals for Hungarian B2B Market
- Adószám / cégjegyzékszám visible on proposals and footers
- Physical office address (even co-working) increases trust significantly
- Hungarian references preferred over international for SME clients
- "Referenciáink" section with logo or company name (with permission)
- Response time commitment: "48 órán belül válaszolunk" builds expectation

## Thought Leadership Copy Rules
- Lead with counterintuitive insight (not "A digitalizáció fontos")
- One concrete case or data point per paragraph
- End with implication: "Ez azt jelenti, hogy..."
- No jargon without immediate plain-language definition
- Own a specific point of view — hedged, neutral copy signals low expertise

## Output Expectations
- About/bio copy (150-300 words)
- Key credibility statements (5 bullet points, each with metric or proof)
- Trust section copy for landing page or proposal

## Quality Criteria
- Every credibility claim verifiable or explicitly marked as approximation
- No generic phrases ("tapasztalt csapat", "minőségi munka") without substantiation
- Reader knows exactly what the expert does and for whom within first 2 sentences
