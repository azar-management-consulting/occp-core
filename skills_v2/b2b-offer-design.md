## Proposal Architecture

**Target:** Hungarian B2B clients, SME to enterprise range

## Document Structure (in order)

**1. Fedőlap (Cover)**
- Client name, project title, proposal date, validity date
- Prepared by: company name, responsible person, contact
- Proposal reference number (for tracking)

**2. Vezetői összefoglaló (Executive Summary)**
- 1 page maximum
- Client situation summary (shows you listened)
- Proposed solution in 2-3 sentences
- Expected outcome with metric
- Investment summary (total price, headline term)

**3. Helyzetértékelés (Situation Analysis)**
- Client's current state (facts confirmed in discovery)
- Identified gap or opportunity
- Risk of inaction (what happens without the project)

**4. Megoldás (Proposed Solution)**
- Methodology name and brief description
- Phase breakdown (Phase 1 / Phase 2 / Phase 3)
- What is included vs. explicitly excluded (scope boundary)

**5. Teljesítési mátrix (Deliverable Matrix)**

| Fázis | Tevékenység | Eredmény | Határidő |
|-------|------------|---------|---------|
| 1 | ... | ... | ... |

**6. Árazás (Pricing)**
- Investment framing first (value, ROI) before price reveal
- Line-item breakdown: each deliverable with price
- Optional add-ons listed separately
- Payment terms: advance % + milestone payments
- Validity: "Az ajánlat [dátum]-ig érvényes"

**7. Referenciák és garanciák**
- 2-3 relevant references (sector + result)
- Satisfaction guarantee or revision policy

**8. Következő lépések**
- Exact action: "Az ajánlat elfogadásához kérjük..."
- Signature line or digital acceptance method

## Pricing Presentation Rules
- Anchor: present highest tier or most comprehensive option first
- ROI framing: "Ez X Ft befektetés, amely Y Ft értéket generál"
- Never apologize for price; present with confidence
- ÁSZF reference: "A részletes feltételek az ÁSZF-ben találhatók"

## Output Expectations
- Complete proposal document (all 8 sections)
- Deliverable matrix populated
- 3-tier pricing structure (if applicable): Alap / Standard / Prémium

## Quality Criteria
- No vague deliverables: every item has measurable completion criteria
- Validity date forces decision (creates urgency without pressure)
- Executive summary standalone readable — decision makers often read only this
